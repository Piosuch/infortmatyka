The History of Volleyball

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA. Originally called "Mintonette," the game was designed as a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball. Over the years, volleyball evolved into the fast-paced and dynamic sport we know today.

Rules and Techniques

The game of volleyball is played between two teams of six players each. The objective is to score points by sending the ball over the net and into the opponent's court without letting it touch the ground on your side. Key techniques include serving, passing, setting, spiking, and blocking.

For detailed rules, visit the FIVB website.

Famous Tournaments and Players

Volleyball has several prestigious tournaments, including the Olympics, the FIVB World Championship, and the Volleyball Nations League. The sport has produced legendary players such as Giba, Karch Kiraly, and Lang Ping.
